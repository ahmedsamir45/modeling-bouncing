Documentation: Euler's Method

Euler's Method

Euler's method is a simple and first-order numerical method for solving ordinary differential equations (ODEs). It uses the following update equations:

Position Update Equation:

The update equation for the position (\(y\)) is:

\[ y_{n+1} = y_n + v_n \cdot \Delta t \] Where: - \(y_n\) is the current height (position), - \(v_n\) is the current velocity, - \(\Delta t\) is the time step.

Velocity Update Equation:

The update equation for the velocity (\(v\)) is:

\[ v_{n+1} = v_n - g \cdot \Delta t \] Where: - \(v_n\) is the current velocity, - \(g\) is the acceleration due to gravity (approximately \(9.81 \, \text{m/s}^2\)), - \(\Delta t\) is the time step.

Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs):

The system we are solving involves the following ODEs for **position** and **velocity**:

\[ \frac{dy}{dt} = v(t) \] \[ \frac{dv}{dt} = -g \] Where: - \(y(t)\) is the position of the object as a function of time, - \(v(t)\) is the velocity of the object as a function of time, - \(g\) is the gravitational acceleration.

Kinetic Energy (KE):

The **kinetic energy** of the object can be calculated using the following equation:

\[ KE = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \] Where: - \(m\) is the mass of the object, - \(v\) is the velocity of the object.

Coefficient of Restitution (Elastic Collision):

When the object hits the ground, the velocity is reversed and scaled by the **coefficient of restitution** (\(r\)), which determines how "bouncy" the object is:

\[ v_{n+1} = -r \cdot v_n \] Where: - \(v_n\) is the velocity just before the bounce, - \(r\) is the coefficient of restitution, which lies between 0 (no bounce) and 1 (perfect bounce).

Summary of Euler's Method Equations:

  • Position Update: \( y_{n+1} = y_n + v_n \cdot \Delta t \)
  • Velocity Update: \( v_{n+1} = v_n - g \cdot \Delta t \)
  • Velocity after bounce: \( v_{n+1} = -r \cdot v_n \)
  • Kinetic Energy: \( KE = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \)
